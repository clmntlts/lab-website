Predictive coding theories propose that the brain minimizes prediction errors by sending only the difference between expected and received signals up the cortical hierarchy. This project rigorously tests this proposal using experimental and theoretical methods.

We develop normative models of predictive coding that account for realistic noise statistics and synaptic constraints, and test their predictions against large-scale imaging and electrophysiology data.

## Current Findings

Our recent work (Brun & Vasquez, PLOS Comput. Biol. 2024) shows that noise correlations in cortical circuits are structured to whiten prediction errors — a signature prediction of our model.
