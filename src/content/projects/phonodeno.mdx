The PhonoDéno project examines the role of phonological awareness and rapid automatized naming (RAN) as predictors and precursors of reading acquisition in French-speaking children. A key strand focuses on training studies in children with dyslexia, testing whether targeted RAN interventions improve reading fluency and phonological processing.

The project combines longitudinal behavioural tracking, neuroimaging before and after training, and randomised controlled trials to provide evidence-based guidance for educational and speech-language pathology practice.
