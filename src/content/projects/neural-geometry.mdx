Sensory cortices do not simply relay information — they transform and compress it according to principles that remain poorly understood. This project asks: **what is the geometric structure of neural representations, and why does that structure matter for computation?**

We develop Riemannian geometric tools for analyzing neural population codes and apply them to recordings from macaque and mouse sensory cortex during naturalistic stimulation. A central hypothesis is that the curvature and topology of neural manifolds are tuned for efficient communication across cortical layers and areas.

## Goals

1. Characterize the geometry of sensory representations across the visual hierarchy
2. Test whether geometric structure predicts inter-area communication efficiency
3. Develop open-source tools for geometric neural data analysis
4. Bridge findings to artificial neural network representations

## Methods

- Neuropixels electrophysiology (up to 1024 channels simultaneously)
- Two-photon calcium imaging
- Riemannian manifold learning and persistent homology
- Comparison with deep convolutional network activations
