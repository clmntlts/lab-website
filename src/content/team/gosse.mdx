Coline Gosse completed her PhD at UCLouvain investigating graphomotor dysfunction in children with dyslexia. Her doctoral work combined behavioural measures of handwriting quality and speed with neuroimaging approaches, revealing altered functional connectivity in the writing network. She continues as a postdoctoral researcher studying the interplay between handwriting, spelling, and reading development.
