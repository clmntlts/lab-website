Claire Gosse completed her PhD at UCLouvain investigating graphomotor dysfunction in children with dyslexia. Her doctoral work combined behavioural measures of handwriting quality and speed with neuroimaging, revealing altered functional connectivity in the writing network. She continues as a postdoctoral researcher studying the interplay between handwriting, spelling, and reading development in typically developing children and those with dyslexia.
