Elena Vasquez is Associate Professor of Computational Neuroscience at the University of Somewhere. She leads the Neural Computation Laboratory, where her team investigates how neural circuits represent, transform, and communicate information.

Before joining the University of Somewhere in 2018, she completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the École Normale Supérieure with the Laboratoire de Neurosciences Cognitives. She received her PhD in Neuroscience from the University of Geneva (2015) and her BSc in Mathematics and Physics from ETH Zurich (2010).

Her work combines experimental recordings in behaving animals with theoretical frameworks from dynamical systems, information theory, and geometric deep learning. A central goal is to understand how the geometry of neural population activity constrains computation — and to use these insights to build more interpretable AI systems.

She is a recipient of the ERC Starting Grant (2023), the SfN Young Investigator Award (2022), and the CNRS Bronze Medal (2019).
